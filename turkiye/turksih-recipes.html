  1. Turkish Kebab

    2. Yields: 4 servings

    Prep time: 30 minutes

    Cook time: 15 minutes

    ingredients

    For The Marinade

    • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
    • 1/2 cup yogurt
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
    • 1 teaspoon paprika
    • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
    • 1/4 teaspoon salt
    • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

    For The Kebabs

    • 12 skewers
    • 1 large onion, cut into thick slices

    Instruction

    1. Marinate the chicken: In a large bowl, combine the yogurt, lemon juice, garlic powder, paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper. Add the chicken pieces and mix well to coat. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to overnight.
    2. Assemble the kebabs: Thread the marinated chicken pieces and onion slices onto the skewers, alternating between the two.
    3. Grill the kebabs: Grill the kebabs over medium heat for 15-20 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the kebabs are nicely charred.
    4. Serve: Serve the kebabs hot with rice, pita bread, or salad.

  2. Baklawa

    3. Yields: 12 servings

    Prep time: 1 hour

    Cook time: 30 minutes

    Ingredients

    • 1 package phyllo dough, thawed
    • 1 cup finely chopped walnuts
    • 1/2 cup finely chopped pistachios
    • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
    • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
    • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
    • 1/2 cup melted butter
    • 1 cup honey
    • 1/4 cup water
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

    Instruction

    1. Prepare the filling: In a bowl, combine the walnuts, pistachios, sugar, cinnamon, and cloves. Mix well.
    2. Prepare the syrup: In a small saucepan, combine the honey, water, and lemon juice. Bring to a boil over medium heat, then reduce heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until thickened. Set aside to cool.
    3. Assemble the baklava: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Butter a 9x13 inch baking pan.
    4. Layer the phyllo dough: Lay one sheet of phyllo dough in the prepared pan, brush with melted butter. Repeat with half of the remaining phyllo sheets.
    5. Add the filling: Spread the nut mixture evenly over the phyllo layers.
    6. Layer the remaining phyllo: Lay the remaining phyllo sheets over the filling, brushing each sheet with melted butter.
    7. Cut into squares: Cut the baklava into desired squares or diamonds.
    8. Bake: Bake for 30-40 minutes, or until golden brown and crisp.
    9. Pour syrup: As soon as the baklava is out of the oven, pour the cooled syrup evenly over the hot baklava.
    10. Let cool: Let the baklava cool completely before serving.
