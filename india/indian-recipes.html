  1. Indian Curry

    2. Yields: 4 servings

    Prep time: 20 minutes

    Cook time: 30 minutes

    ingredients

    • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
    • 1 tablespoon yogurt
    • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1 teaspoon garam masala
    • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 1 large onion, chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 inch ginger, grated
    • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
    • 1 cup coconut milk
    • 1 cup water
    • 1 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter)
    • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

    Instruction

    1. Marinate the chicken: In a large bowl, combine the yogurt, turmeric, garam masala, cumin, chili powder, and salt. Add the chicken pieces and mix well to coat. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to overnight.
    2. Cook the onions and spices: Heat the ghee in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onions and cook until softened and golden brown. Stir in the garlic and ginger, and cook for 1-2 minutes more.
    3. Add the tomatoes and spices: Add the diced tomatoes, turmeric, garam masala, cumin, and chili powder to the pot. Cook for 10-15 minutes, or until the tomatoes have broken down and the sauce has thickened.
    4. Add the chicken and coconut milk: Add the marinated chicken pieces to the pot. Pour in the coconut milk and water. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to low and cook for 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened.
    5. Serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with rice or naan bread.

  2. Indian Biryani

    3. Yields: 4 servings

    Prep time: 30 minutes

    Cook time: 45 minutes

    ingredients

    For The Marinad

    • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
    • 1 tablespoon yogurt
    • 1 teaspoon garam masala
    • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
    • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder

    For The Biryani

    • 2 cups basmati rice, washed and soaked in water for 30 minutes
    • 1 large onion, finely chopped
    • 1 inch ginger, grated
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
    • 1 cup coconut milk
    • 1 cup water
    • 1 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter)
    • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

    Instruction

    1. Marinate the chicken: In a large bowl, combine the yogurt, turmeric, garam masala, cumin, chili powder, and salt. Add the chicken pieces and mix well to coat. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to overnight.
    2. Cook the rice: Drain the soaked rice and cook according to package directions.
    3. Prepare the biryani mixture: Heat the ghee in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onions and cook until softened and golden brown. Stir in the garlic and ginger, and cook for 1-2 minutes more.
    4. Add the tomatoes: Add the diced tomatoes to the pot and cook for 10-15 minutes, or until the tomatoes have broken down and the sauce has thickened.
    5. Add the chicken and coconut milk: Add the marinated chicken pieces to the pot. Pour in the coconut milk and water. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to low and cook for 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened.
    6. Layer the biryani: In a large pot or baking dish, layer the cooked rice, chicken, and sauce. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used.
    7. Cook the biryani: Seal the pot with foil or a tight-fitting lid. Cook in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for 20-25 minutes, or until the biryani is heated through and the flavors have blended.
    8. Serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with rice or naan bread.
